    What is a Bachelor of Physiotherapy (BPT)?

    BPT stands for Bachelor of Physiotherapy. Physiotherapy is the process carried out to maintain and restore maximum movement and functional ability throughout the lifespan of an individual. Physiotherapists thoroughly understand human anatomy and the intricacies of bones and muscles etc. Students who wish to pursue a career as physiotherapists can take the BPT course. This course teaches physical movement, post-fracture maneuvers, injury, chronic pain, and hip pains.

    Why Pursue BPT in 2023?

    • An In-demand Occupation with future scope
    • Offers rewardable salaries to well-educated and experienced physiotherapists

    Under-Graduate

    BPT - An Overview

    BPT is a four-year undergraduate program including a 6 months clinical Internship Program wherein students learn to deal with the physical movement of the body aiming to cure disorders of the patients. During the course, the students develop an understanding of the human body on the basis of medical science.

    BPT Specializations

    Orthopedic Physiotherapy:

    It deals with the musculoskeletal system which includes bones, muscles, ligaments, tendons, joints, and connective tissue and deals with arthritis, bursitis, cancer, frozen shoulder, knee instability, joint pain, limited range of motion, lower back pain, etc.

    Neurological Physiotherapy:

    It deals with functional difficulties resulting from the nerve and neuromuscular systems of the body like loss of function, diminished sensitivity, uncontrollable muscle spasms, etc.

    Pediatrics Physiotherapy:

    It deals with the evaluation, recognition, diagnosis, and treatment of movement abnormalities encountered in children such as Orthopedics, congenital defects, neurology, neuropsychiatry, respiration, prematurity, etc.

    Geriatrics Physiotherapy:

    It deals with treating illnesses affecting older persons such as osteoporosis and arthritis, etc.

    Eligibility Criteria

    The Students must have passed 10+2 with Physics, Chemistry, and Biology as compulsory subjects with a minimum of 50% marks from a recognized institute.

    The Students should have completed the age of 17 years at the time of admission

    Post-Graduate

    MPT - An Overview

    MPT is a postgraduate program that focuses on a special type of therapy to treat and control various diseases and disorders in humans. Unlike the MBBS, it focuses on the treatment of various human disorders with certain types of therapies and exercises.

    MPT Specializations

    Cardio-pulmonary Sciences:

    It deals with the diagnosis and research of coronary illnesses, artery diseases, congenital heart defects, and valvular heart disorders.

    Community Physiotherapy:

    It deals with primary care, clinical management, health promotion, and expanded physiotherapy skills.

    Neurosciences:

    It deals with knowledge of neuroscience and those afflicted with connected diseases.

    Orthopedics:

    It deals with the treatment of the musculoskeletal system or trauma to the muscles and bones in the body

    Pediatrics and Women's Health:

    It deals with patients with gynecologic and obstetrical disorders.

    Eligibility Criteria

    The Students must have passed 10+2 with Physics, Chemistry, and Biology as compulsory subjects with a minimum of 50% marks from a recognized institute.

    The Students must have completed BPT from a recognized university with 50% marks in addition to a 6-month internship in the same core domain.
